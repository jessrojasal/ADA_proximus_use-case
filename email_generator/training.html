Phishing Prevention Guide

Best Practices for Employees

  • Verify Email Senders
    • Always check the email domain and sender address.
    • Be cautious of emails with generic greetings like "Dear Customer."
    • Inspect the email headers to trace its origin.
  • Recognize Phishing Signs
    • Look out for urgent or threatening language in emails.
    • Check for poor grammar, misspellings, or unusual formatting.
    • Avoid opening suspicious attachments, especially .exe, .zip, or .rar files.
  • Secure Your Credentials
    • Never share your password via email or text.
    • Use a password manager for secure storage.
    • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.
  • Beware of Fake Websites
    • Always check for HTTPS and the correct URL before logging in.
    • Be cautious of look-alike domains that mimic real websites.
    • Use a VPN when accessing sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi.
  • Report Suspicious Emails
    • If you receive a suspicious email, report it to the IT security team immediately.
    • Do not click on links or download attachments from unknown sources.
    • Use email platforms' built-in options to report phishing emails.
  • Additional Preventative Measures
    • Regularly review your financial accounts for unauthorized transactions.
    • Install and update anti-malware software.
    • Keep your operating system and applications updated.
    • Verify unexpected communications through official channels.
